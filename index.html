Thingyan

Myanmar Thingyan Festival


Thingyan is the Myanmar New Year festival that usually occurs in middle of April. It is a Buddhist festival celebrated over a period of four to five days, culminating in the New Year.

Golden Padauk Flower


Golden Padauk flower are blossoming due to the first rain shower nationwide, signifying the upcoming traditional Thingyan water festival in Myanmar. The Padauk flower is well-known for its blooming period only in April when the country's traditional Thingyan water festival falls. The Padauk flowers are currently seen almost everywhere across the country.

Mont Lone Yay Paw

Mont Lone Yay Paw are rice balls filled with plain jaggery or palm sugar. Family members or friends gather together and make Mont Lone Yay Paw happily during the festival.

Shwe Yin Aye

Shwe Yin Aye is another traditional dessert associated with Thingyan Festival. It is made by combining steamed sticky rice, tapioca pearls, tapioca sticks, cendol jelly noodles,coconut jelly, sugar syrup and chilled coconut milk. Shwe Yin Aye is served with ice cubes and a slice of plain bread is often added to the combination.

Burmese Sticky Rice

Burmese sticky rice is a glutinous rice-based savory snack, and a seasonal festive delicacy in Myanmar. This is said to be the most favorite meal of Myanmar People.

Thingyan Rice

Thingyan rice is infused with water and commonly served with a salad of cured salted fish, which is blanched and fried with onions, along with sour mango or marian plum.
